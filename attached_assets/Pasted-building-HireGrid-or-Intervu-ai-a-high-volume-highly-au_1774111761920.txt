But unlike standard ATS platforms (like Lever or Greenhouse) that just move cards across a Kanban board, you are building a Supply Chain Engine for Hiring.

Here is the complete, detailed breakdown of the system architecture, the user journeys, and the core philosophies we’ve coded into the platform.

🏛️ The Core Philosophy: Supply Meets Demand
The entire platform revolves around balancing two sides of a marketplace:

Supply (Interviewers/Panelists): Time slots provided by human engineers/managers to conduct interviews.

Demand (Candidates): Applicants who have passed initial screening and need to be interviewed for specific rounds.

The system’s primary job is to ensure you never have a "Capacity Deficit" (e.g., 50 candidates waiting for Round 1, but only 10 interview slots available).

🧩 The 3 Main Pillars of the Platform
1. The "Headless" Panelist Engine (Supply)
This is your platform's superpower. External interviewers (or busy internal engineers) do not need to create accounts or log in.

The Invite: A Recruiter adds a Panelist to a Program and assigns them to specific constraints (e.g., "Sarah can only take Round 1 and Round 2").

The Magic Link: The system generates a highly secure, unique URL (/availability/[token]).

The Scheduler: When Sarah clicks the link, she sees a beautiful, mobile-friendly interface.

Smart Time Snapping: If Sarah's assigned round is 45 minutes, the UI forces her to add availability in 45-minute blocks. This prevents "phantom capacity" (e.g., offering a 30-minute block for a 45-minute interview, which is useless).

Conflict Prevention: The UI actively blocks overlapping time slots.

2. The Candidate Pipeline (Demand)
We moved away from a messy Kanban board to a scalable, data-dense list view.

The Inbox (Drafts): Candidates enter the system (via manual upload, CSV, or career page) as DRAFT. Here, an ATS AI scans their resume and gives them a Match Score (e.g., 85%).

Bulk Processing: The Recruiter selects the top candidates and clicks "Shortlist & Invite".

Dynamic States: The candidates are moved to ACTIVE status and assigned an activeRoundId (e.g., mapped to Round 1).

Self-Scheduling: The system emails the candidate their own Magic Link (/book/[token]). The candidate clicks it and picks a time slot from the aggregated pool of Panelist availability.

3. The Control Tower (The Recruiter Dashboard)
This is the UI we just perfected. It gives the Recruiter total visibility and control over the hiring drive.

Global Filters: The Recruiter can view the health of the entire program or drill down into a specific stage (e.g., "Show me the capacity for the System Design round").

Health Badges: The system calculates Supply - Demand. If Demand outpaces Supply, the dashboard flashes red alerts notifying the recruiter of a bottleneck.

Granular Table: Shows every interviewer, exactly how many usable slots they have provided, and a progress bar of their capacity.

God Mode (Admin Sheet): If a panelist messages the recruiter saying, "I'm free tomorrow at 2 PM," the recruiter can click "Manage Time" and input the availability on the panelist's behalf.

One-Click Reminders: A single button triggers a server action that emails a panelist their magic link again, nudging them to add more slots.

🗄️ The Data Architecture (Prisma Schema Recap)
We redesigned the database to be extremely robust:

User & Organization: The B2B layer. Recruiters and Admins belong here.

Program & Round: The configuration. A Program (e.g., "SDE-2 Hiring") has multiple Rounds. Rounds are fully dynamic (you can have 2 rounds or 10 rounds, with custom durations).

ProgramPanelist: The crucial pivot table. It links a Panelist to a Program, stores their assignedRoundIds, their secure magicLink, and their raw availableSlots as JSON.

Candidate: Stores the applicant data, their ATS score, their current activeRoundId, and their own bookingToken.

🚀 The User Journey (How it all connects)
Here is a day in the life of a hiring drive on your platform:

Setup: Recruiter creates "Frontend Developer" Program and defines 2 Rounds: "React Coding" (60m) and "Culture Fit" (30m).

Staffing: Recruiter goes to the Panelist Manager. They invite Alice (assigned to Coding) and Bob (assigned to Culture Fit).

Supply Generation: Alice and Bob get their Magic Links. Alice adds 5 hours of availability. Bob adds 2 hours.

Sourcing: 100 resumes are uploaded. The ATS scores them.

Shortlisting: Recruiter goes to the Candidate Inbox, selects the 10 candidates with an ATS score > 80%, and bulk-moves them to "React Coding".

Demand Spike: The Control Tower instantly updates. Demand for the Coding round is now 10. Supply (from Alice) is only 5 slots.

Bottleneck Alert: The Control Tower flashes RED: "5 Slot Deficit".

Resolution: Recruiter clicks the "Mail" icon next to Alice to remind her to add time, or invites a new engineer, Charlie, to help with the Coding round.

Booking: Candidates receive their emails, click their links, and consume the slots provided by Alice and Charlie.
